# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using SQL

## Project Overview
This project focuses on performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using **MySQL** on a data warehouse.

The goal is to understand business performance, customer behaviour, product trends, and revenue distribution by answering real-world business questions using SQL queries.

---

## Tools Used
- MySQL (Workbench)
- SQL (CTEs, JOins, Aggregations, Window Functions)
- Jupyter Notebook (for CSV ingestion)

---

### 1. Database Exploration
- Explored schema, tables, and columns
- Understood data warehouse structure

### 2. Dimension Exploration
- Customer geography analysis
- Product category exploration

### 3. Date Exploration
- First and last order dates
- Sales coverage period (years of data)

### 5. Magnitude Analysis
- Revenue by category
- Customers by country and gender

### 6. Ranking Analysis
- Top & bottom products by revenue
- Top 10 customers by revenue
- Customers with the fewest orders

---

## Key Insights
### Business Performance
- Total Sales: ~29.36M
- Total Orders: ~27.6k
- Total Quantity Sold: ~60.4k
- Average Selling Price: ~486

### Product Insights
- Bike dominates revenue, contributing the majority of total sales.
- Accessories and Clothing generate significantly lower revenue.
- The Mountain-200 product series consistently ranks among the top revenue-generating products.

### Customer & Geography Insights
- The United States contributes the highest share of sales (~34%).
- Australia and the UK are strong secondary markets.
- Sales are geographically concentrated, indicating potential expansion opportunities.

### Cost & Category Insights
- Bikes have the highest average cost, confirming a premium product category.
- Accessories and clothing act as low-cost add-ons.
- Some NULL categories highlight data quality improvement opportunities.
